Discover Swat Valley

The Switzerland of the East

Swat Valley is a place of fairy tales, majestic mountains, and crystal-clear waters. Located in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan, it is renowned for its outstanding natural beauty.

From the snow-capped peaks of Malam Jabba to the serene waters of Mahodand Lake, Swat offers a diverse range of experiences for every traveler. Whether you seek adventure, history, or tranquility, Swat has it all.

A Legacy of Kings

Swat is not just about nature; it's a land deep in history. Once a major center of early Buddhism, the valley is dotted with ancient stupas and monasteries. The region also boasts a proud Pashtun heritage, known for the unwritten code of "Pashtunwali" which emphasizes hospitality and honor.


Local handicrafts, especially the famous Swati embroidery and wood carving, are testaments to the artistic skills of the people. Festivals are celebrated with traditional music and dance, making every visit a cultural immersion.

Top Attractions

MUST-VISIT DESTINATIONS

Malam Jabba

The only ski resort in Pakistan, offering snowboarding, skiing, and chairlift rides with breathtaking views.

Mahodand Lake

A glacial alpine lake located in the upper Ushu Valley, famous for boating and fishing amidst towering pines.

Kalam Valley

Known for its waterfalls, thick forests, and the swirling Swat River, Kalam is the heart of tourism in the region.

White Palace

The historic summer residence of the Wali of Swat, built with white marble in 1940, now a heritage hotel.
